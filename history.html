Quick Overview

History of Tai Chi and Qigong

Qigong

Qigong is a broad Chinese mind-body practice that combines gentle movement, breathing, posture, and focused awareness. Its roots reach back thousands of years through Chinese health, meditation, martial, and spiritual traditions. The word is often understood as cultivating or working with life energy through steady practice.

Tai Chi

Tai Chi, also called Taijiquan, developed as an internal Chinese martial art. Over time, many people began practicing it for balance, relaxation, coordination, and general wellness. Its slow, flowing movements are often described as meditation in motion.

How They Connect

Tai Chi and Qigong overlap in many ways. Both emphasize relaxed posture, smooth breathing, body awareness, and calm focus. Qigong is usually simpler and more modular, while Tai Chi often follows longer movement forms. Chair-based practice adapts these ideas so people can explore gentle movement safely while seated.

Stillwater Approach

Stillwater is not a replacement for an instructor, medical care, or physical therapy. It is a peaceful guided practice space designed to encourage stillness, breath, and accessible seated movement.
