Chair Tai Chi
Workout Plan
A 28-Day Seated Program — Companion Guide

🪑 Your Chair

Use a sturdy, armless chair with a firm seat. Feet should rest flat on the floor. Avoid chairs with wheels. Keep open space around you so your arms can extend fully in all directions.

🌬 Breathe First

Inhale as you open, expand, or lift. Exhale as you close, contract, or lower. Breath drives movement in Tai Chi — never hold it. If you feel dizzy, pause and breathe normally.

🐢 Move Slowly

All movements should be slow, continuous, and deliberate. There is no hurrying. Quality of movement matters far more than speed or the number of repetitions completed.

休 Honor Rest Days

Rest days are not gaps in the program — they are part of it. Use them to notice how your body feels. Light walking is welcome. Stillness and integration are the point.

⏱ How Long

Week 1–2 sessions run approximately 15 minutes. Week 3–4 sessions run 20–25 minutes. Start your timer only after you are seated, settled, and composed.

⚠ Safety First

Consult your physician before beginning this or any exercise program. Stop and rest immediately if you feel pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath. Every exercise can be modified.
